# Key Podcasts on B2B Email Marketing

**Research area:** B2B SaaS email marketing strategy and execution  
**Last reviewed:** June 21, 2026  
**Selection criteria:** Must feature recurring, substantive discussion of email marketing strategy or newsletter operations in B2B contexts. Actively publishing as of 2026.

---

## 1. Exit 5 Podcast — Dave Gerhardt

**Host:** Dave Gerhardt (formerly CMO at Drift, co-founder of Privy)  
**Feed:** Available on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and at exit5.com  
**Frequency:** Multiple episodes per week  
**Format:** Interviews with B2B marketers and founders + live session replays (including email-specific deep-dives with panelists sharing real campaigns)

**Why it matters for email research:**  
Exit 5 is the most direct source of practitioner-level email marketing content in B2B. Dave runs twice-monthly live sessions exclusively on B2B marketing topics — email is one of the most recurring subjects — and publishes the recordings as podcast episodes. These sessions feature 3–5 marketers each sharing a specific campaign or email tactic in detail, with real numbers.

The episodes most relevant to this research are the "live session" format, where panelists share screen recordings of actual email campaigns, discuss open/reply rates, and explain the operational model behind their newsletter or lifecycle program. This is the kind of specificity almost never found in solo-host episodes.

**Best episodes to start with:**  
Search for "email" in the Exit 5 feed. The live session format (multiple speakers, campaign teardowns) is consistently more actionable than interview episodes.

**Connection to this research:**  
Multiple transcript files in `/research/youtube-transcripts/dave-gerhardt/` are drawn from Exit 5 content. The newsjacking system, newsletter trust frameworks, and reply-rate metric discussion in [insights/emerging-trends.md](../../insights/emerging-trends.md) all originated in Exit 5 sessions.

## 2. The Bootstrapped Founder — Arvid Kahl

**Host:** Arvid Kahl (author of Zero to Sold, newsletter operator, SaaS founder)  
**Feed:** Available on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and at thebootstrappedfounder.com  
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Format:** Long-form interviews with bootstrapped founders and indie creators, recurring focus on audience-building and newsletter operations

**Why it matters for email research:**  
Arvid Kahl built his own bootstrapped SaaS, sold it, and then built an audience-first business through his newsletter and podcast — giving him firsthand experience on both the product and distribution sides of SaaS email. His interview with Brennan Dunn (transcribed in this research archive) covers advanced email personalization, segmentation architecture, and the technical reality of building behavioral email at scale inside RightMessage.

The podcast sits at the intersection of technical founder thinking and email marketing strategy — useful for B2B SaaS companies building email programs without large marketing teams, where the founder or a small team is doing both product and marketing.

**Best episodes to start with:**  
The Brennan Dunn episode on email personalization is directly transcribed in `/research/youtube-transcripts/brennan-dunn/mastering-email-marketing-brennan-dunn.md`. Other strong episodes: any featuring newsletter monetization, audience-building frameworks, or email deliverability.

**Connection to this research:**  
Brennan Dunn transcripts in this archive came from Bootstrapped Founder episodes. The behavioral segmentation trend in [insights/emerging-trends.md](../../insights/emerging-trends.md) draws heavily from this source.

## 3. Executive Brand Podcast — Finn Thørmer

**Host:** Finn Thørmer (B2B executive brand strategist)  
**Feed:** Available on Spotify and Apple Podcasts  
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Format:** Long-form interviews with B2B CEOs, executives, and founders on thought leadership and audience growth

**Why it matters for email research:**  
The Emily Kramer episode (transcribed in this archive) is one of the most detailed public conversations about running a B2B newsletter as a primary business — covering growth mechanics, content strategy, platform decisions (Substack vs. Beehiiv), AI integration into the production workflow, and the ecosystem marketing playbook that underlies newsletter growth.

While the show isn't exclusively about email, the Kramer episode sets a benchmark for how senior B2B marketing practitioners are thinking about newsletters in 2026: as a product, not a channel, with its own editorial model, subscriber metrics, and monetization logic.

**Best episodes to start with:**  
The Emily Kramer episode is transcribed in full at `/research/youtube-transcripts/emily-kramer/how-to-grow-newsletter-mkt1-emily-kramer.md`. The interview covers newsletter growth from 2.6K to 75K+ subscribers, AI workflow integration, and the Substack platform critique.

**Connection to this research:**  
Emily Kramer transcripts in this archive came from Executive Brand Podcast episodes. The editorial newsletter trend and ecosystem marketing framework in [insights/emerging-trends.md](../../insights/emerging-trends.md) draw directly from this source.
