# Key B2B SaaS Email Newsletters Worth Following

**Research area:** B2B SaaS email marketing strategy and execution  
**Last reviewed:** June 21, 2026  
**Selection criteria:** Must cover email/newsletter strategy directly, run by a practitioner with verifiable B2B SaaS operator experience, actively publishing as of 2026.

---

## 1. MKT1 Newsletter — Emily Kramer

**URL:** https://www.mkt1.co  
**Platform:** Substack  
**Frequency:** Bi-weekly  
**Subscribers:** 75,000+ (as of mid-2026)  
**Focus:** Long-form marketing strategy for B2B startups — covering channels, team structure, hiring, and go-to-market frameworks

**Why follow:**  
Emily Kramer was VP Marketing at Carta (scaled from 300 to 850 people), head of marketing at Asana (1 to 25-person team), and at Astro (acquired by Slack). Her newsletters are unusually dense — original diagrams, structured frameworks, and practitioner-level specificity that's rarely available publicly. She writes from experience building marketing functions from scratch, which means her advice on email programs is grounded in what actually gets resourced and shipped inside growth-stage B2B companies.

Notable: 30–40% of her Substack subscriber growth comes from the cross-recommendation network, making her one of the best examples of ecosystem-driven newsletter growth in B2B marketing.

**Best issues to start with:**  
Search her archive for "email," "newsletter," and "ecosystem marketing."

## 2. Exit 5 Newsletter — Dave Gerhardt

**URL:** https://www.exit5.com  
**Platform:** Proprietary (Exit 5 community)  
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Focus:** B2B marketing tactics, what's working right now, candid takes from a practitioner who built marketing at Drift and Privy

**Why follow:**  
Dave Gerhardt is one of the highest-signal voices on B2B marketing execution. He co-founded Privy (email-focused e-commerce tool) and was CMO at Drift. His newsletter is structured as a peer-to-peer communication — Dave writes it himself, replies to readers personally, and tracks reply rates as his primary quality signal. He regularly shares in-depth examples from his community's live sessions (which he records and publishes), giving his newsletter unusually deep coverage of what practitioners are actually doing with email right now.

The Exit 5 community (paid) runs twice-monthly live sessions where 200+ B2B marketers share email campaigns in detail — the newsletter is the distribution layer that keeps the community connected between sessions.

**Best issues to start with:**  
His "email is not dead" issues and anything tagged with campaign teardowns.

## 3. Dear Marketer — Kath Pay

**URL:** https://holisticemailmarketing.com/dear-marketer  
**Platform:** Holistic Email Marketing website / direct subscribe  
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Focus:** Email strategy and psychology — customer-centric frameworks, deliverability, behavioral science applied to email copy

**Why follow:**  
Kath Pay has 27+ years of email marketing experience, is an Amazon bestselling author (Holistic Email Marketing), and is CEO of Holistic Email Marketing, which trains brands including eBay and Tommy Hilfiger. She is one of the few email practitioners who explicitly connects behavioral psychology (Cialdini's persuasion principles, cognitive biases, bimodal buyer personas) to email copywriting decisions — and she tests these approaches empirically with clients.

Her newsletter directly applies to the craft level of B2B email: subject lines, copy structure, CTA language, and when to use which persuasion lever. She was also an early adopter of training private AI models on email expertise, making her useful for understanding how senior practitioners are integrating AI into email strategy.

**Best issues to start with:**  
Issues on inbox placement psychology, deliverability signals, and her "customer-centric" framework series.

## 4. Growth Unhinged — Kyle Poyar

**URL:** https://www.growthunhinged.com  
**Platform:** Substack  
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Focus:** Product-led growth (PLG) strategy — pricing, free-to-paid conversion, SaaS growth benchmarks

**Why follow:**  
Kyle Poyar is Operating Partner at OpenView and the most cited voice on PLG in B2B SaaS. While Growth Unhinged isn't exclusively an email marketing newsletter, it's essential reading for email marketers at PLG companies because it covers the underlying mechanics that email programs serve: trial-to-paid conversion rates, activation benchmarks, expansion revenue triggers, and pricing changes. His quarterly SaaS benchmarks reports are widely cited across the industry.

For email marketers, Growth Unhinged answers the strategic "why are we sending this email?" questions: what signals indicate a user is ready to convert, when does retention email lift LTV, and how should free-to-paid upgrade flows be structured.

**Best issues to start with:**  
His PLG benchmarks reports and any issue covering onboarding email or activation.

## 5. The Steal Club / Really Good Emails Digest

**URL:** https://reallygoodemails.com  
**Platform:** Direct subscribe  
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Focus:** Curated collection of best-in-class email design and copy from real brands across B2B and B2C

**Why follow:**  
Really Good Emails is the industry's primary reference library for email design and copywriting patterns. The newsletter surfaces examples of emails that are actually working — with full design renders, subject lines, and sender context. For B2B SaaS email marketers, it's a reliable way to build a swipe file without manually subscribing to hundreds of lists.

Particularly useful for: onboarding email sequences, trial expiration copy, product announcement formats, win-back campaigns, and newsletter design patterns. Kath Pay's company (Holistic Email Marketing) acquired Really Good Emails in 2024, integrating it with their 15,000+ template archive under Beefree.

**Best issues to start with:**  
Filter their archive by "B2B" or "SaaS" for the most directly applicable examples.
