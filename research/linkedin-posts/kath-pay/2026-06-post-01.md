# The biggest problem usually isn't the copy. Or the design. Or even the strategy.

**Author:** Kath Pay
**Date:** June 20, 2026

## Content

I've analysed thousands of email campaigns over the years. And the thing that keeps showing up? The biggest problem usually isn't the copy. Or the design. Or even the strategy.

It's that brands stop thinking like customers.

Emails become internal. Brand-centric. Broadcasty. Disconnected from what real people actually care about.

The best-performing campaigns tend to do something much simpler, and much harder: They meet people where they already are. Clear offer. Clear value. Clear next step.

Not hacks. Not tricks. Just better thinking.

## Key Insight for B2B SaaS Email

After analysing thousands of campaigns, Kath's finding is that the root failure is perspective drift — emails start serving the sender's agenda (product launches, feature announcements, quarterly targets) rather than the subscriber's actual situation. For B2B SaaS, the fix is a single editorial question before every send: "What does this person need to know right now, and why does it matter to their job?"
