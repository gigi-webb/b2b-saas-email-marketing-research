## Content

6 words from Alex M H Smith changed how I'm building the next version of Unignorable.

Because this isn't the first time Alex has rewired how I think. A few years ago, I flew to California to meet my Unignorable co-founders. Alex argued if you couldn't explain your strategy in a single sentence, you might not have one.

So somewhere over the Rockies, I tried it on Unignorable. What came out was a phrase I've used ever since: a confidence factory.

## Key Insight for B2B SaaS Email

A single positioning sentence — "a confidence factory" — makes every email decision easier because it filters for fit: does this content build confidence in the reader, or does it serve the brand's agenda? B2B SaaS email programs that can state their purpose in one sentence (e.g. "we make [ICP] better at [job to be done]") have a reliable editorial compass that prevents the drift toward brand-centric messaging Kath Pay identifies as the root cause of underperforming campaigns.
