## The Core Problem This Playbook Solves

Most B2B SaaS companies treat email as a broadcast channel.
They send product updates, feature announcements, and
promotional offers to their entire list on a fixed schedule.

Result: declining open rates, rising unsubscribes, and a
list that stops trusting the sender.

This playbook treats email as a relationship system.
The goal is not to send more emails. The goal is to send
the right email to the right person at the right moment
in their lifecycle.

## Playbook 01 — B2B SaaS Newsletter

### Goal
Build trust and authority while generating warm pipeline
through consistent presence.

### Cadence
Weekly. Same day, same time.
Consistency builds habit for both sender and reader.

### Structure
Evidence: Dave Gerhardt + Gaetano DiNardi transcripts

1. Personal Note (100-200 words)
   Written to one person, not a list.
   Peer talking to peer, not brand to customer.

2. Core Insight (200-400 words)
   One idea, explored deeply.
   Not a roundup. Not a list of links.
   Something the reader cannot find with a Google search.

3. Resource or Example (100 words)
   One thing: article, tool, case study, or data point.
   Curated, not aggregated.

4. Soft CTA (50 words)
   One ask. Never two.
   Low friction: reply, click, or share.
   Never a hard sell in a trust-building newsletter.

### Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Reply Rate | >2% | Highest intent signal |
| Click-to-Open Rate | >15% | Content relevance |
| Unsubscribe Rate | <0.3% | List health |
| List Growth Rate | >5% MoM | Top of funnel |

Do not optimize for open rate alone.
Apple Mail Privacy Protection has made open rate
unreliable as a primary metric since 2021.

### What to Avoid
- Sending to inactive subscribers without re-engagement first
- Using no-reply sender addresses
- Writing in "we" language instead of "you" language
- Treating every issue as a product announcement

## Playbook 02 — SaaS Onboarding Email Sequence

### Goal
Convert trial users to paid by demonstrating value
before the trial expires.

### Sequence Structure
Evidence: Val Geisler transcripts

Day 0 — Welcome
Single CTA: complete one key action.
Plain text. Signed by a real person.

Day 1 — Value Demonstration
Show what success looks like with the product.
One real customer story or data point.

Day 3 — Check-In
"Did you get a chance to try X?"
Remove friction: offer help, not a pitch.

Day 7 — Social Proof
One customer story relevant to their use case.
Show the outcome, not the features.

Day 14 — Obstacle Removal
"What is getting in the way?"
Offer a call, a resource, or a direct answer.

Day 21 — Upgrade Prompt (freemium only)
Clear value summary: what they have accomplished.
What they unlock with paid.

### Key Principle
Evidence: Val Geisler retention-email-tactics transcript

The question is never "why aren't they upgrading?"
The question is "what is stopping them from getting value?"
Fix the value gap first. The upgrade follows.

## Playbook 03 — Re-engagement Sequence

### Goal
Identify which inactive subscribers are worth keeping
and remove the rest before they damage deliverability.

### Trigger
Subscriber has not opened or clicked in 90 days.

### Sequence

Email 1 — The Check-In
Subject: "Still there?"
Acknowledge the silence. One question. Plain text only.

Email 2 — The Value Reminder (7 days later)
Subject: "In case you missed it"
Your single best piece of content from last 90 days.

Email 3 — The Exit (7 days later)
Subject: "Removing you from the list"
Tell them they will be removed in 7 days.
Give one click to stay subscribed.
This email typically has highest engagement of the three.

### Why This Matters
Evidence: Kath Pay holistic-email-marketing transcript

A SaaS company with chronic deliverability problems was
emailing people who had not engaged in 5 years. Solution
was not technical. It was strategic: stop emailing people
who are not interested. Deliverability improved within
30 days of list cleanup.

## Playbook 04 — Lifecycle Email Map

### The Six Stages

Stage 1: Subscriber (not yet a user)
Goal: move to trial or demo
Email type: Newsletter and educational content

Stage 2: Trial User (Day 0-14)
Goal: reach activation milestone
Email type: Onboarding sequence (Playbook 02)

Stage 3: Activated User
Goal: build habit, drive expansion
Email type: Tips, advanced features, case studies

Stage 4: Power User
Goal: convert to annual, expand seats
Email type: ROI reports, expansion offers, referral

Stage 5: At-Risk User (declining engagement)
Goal: prevent churn
Email type: Check-in, obstacle removal, success story

Stage 6: Churned User
Goal: win back or learn
Email type: Win-back sequence and exit survey
