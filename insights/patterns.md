# Patterns — B2B SaaS Newsletter & Email Marketing

**Source:** Analysis of 14 YouTube transcripts from 8 experts
**Last Updated:** June 21, 2026
**Method:** Cross-referencing themes across multiple practitioners

---

## Pattern 01 — Email is the only channel you truly own

**Frequency:** 6/8 experts mentioned this explicitly

**Evidence:**
- Dave Gerhardt (Exit Five transcript): "Email is still one of the
  last channels that you can truly own your audience."
- Kath Pay (Holistic Email Marketing transcript): Email has evolved
  from campaign-based to subscriber-based — the channel itself
  has matured toward ownership thinking.
- Val Geisler: Retention email is the primary lever for reducing
  churn — not paid ads, not social.

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
Newsletter and lifecycle email should be treated as owned
infrastructure, not a campaign tactic. SaaS companies that
treat email as a broadcast channel will lose to those who
treat it as a relationship system.

---

## Pattern 02 — Write to one person, not a list

**Frequency:** 5/8 experts mentioned this directly

**Evidence:**
- Dave Gerhardt (Exit Five transcript): "Write right to one person.
  Right to one person is always the good approach."
- Kath Pay: "A one-on-one communication. That literally is how
  I structure the newsletters I'm producing right now."
- Gaetano DiNardi: Personal note section in newsletter — picture,
  name, title — before any promotional content.

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
Email copy should feel like a peer talking to a peer. The moment
it reads like a press release, engagement drops. Subject lines,
opening lines, and CTAs should be written as if sent to a
single person you know.

---

## Pattern 03 — Give value before you ask for anything

**Frequency:** 7/8 experts aligned on this

**Evidence:**
- Kath Pay: "Earn the right to expect engagement. Give away tons
  of free value before you expect anything in return."
- Dave Gerhardt: "I assume that nobody wants my marketing and
  therefore I'm going to work with that as the key factor."
- Kath Pay: "You-focused language. Instead of 'we are hosting
  this event', say 'you will learn this'."

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
B2B SaaS email programs that lead with product promotion
before establishing value will see high unsubscribe rates.
The sequence must be: value → trust → conversion, not
conversion → value → trust.

---

## Pattern 04 — Consistency beats frequency

**Frequency:** 5/8 experts mentioned

**Evidence:**
- Dave Gerhardt (Exit Five transcript): "Whether it's ramping up
  to send more emails but being consistent with it, or scaling
  down to send fewer — don't just send salesy emails, show up
  consistently."
- Kath Pay: Email strategy must be a "living, breathing document"
  with regular reviews — not a one-time setup.

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
A weekly newsletter sent consistently for 12 months outperforms
a daily newsletter sent for 3 months then abandoned. Consistency
builds the habit for readers and builds sender reputation with
email providers.

## Pattern 05 — Email lurkers are buyers in disguise

**Frequency:** Mentioned by 3/8 experts, but highly specific insight

**Evidence:**
- Dave Gerhardt (Exit Five transcript): "There are many lurkers
  who open your emails, read them, never engage. But all of these
  touchpoints through newsletters add value to the final decision
  if a person is going to become a buyer or not."
- Exit Five case study: Tracked all buyers and checked how many
  emails they had opened in the 10 weeks before purchase.
  Result: email engagement predicted purchase intent even without
  clicks on CTAs.

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
Open rate and CTR alone are misleading metrics. Dark social
email engagement — people reading but not clicking — is a
significant driver of eventual conversion. Attribution models
that only count last-click will undervalue email.

---

## Pattern 06 — Strategy is not a list of tactics

**Frequency:** Explicit in 3/8 experts, implied in most others

**Evidence:**
- Kath Pay: "Strategy starts with business goals → marketing
  objectives → lifecycle stage objectives → tactics. Most people
  call a list of tactics a strategy. That is not a strategy."
- Kath Pay: "Without a written strategy, when you leave the
  company, everything leaves with you. The team is left
  floundering."

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
Most B2B SaaS email programs are collections of disconnected
tactics. A true email strategy ties every send to a lifecycle
stage objective, which ties to a measurable business goal.
Documentation is not optional — it is part of the strategy.

---

## Pattern 07 — Customer-centric email reduces deliverability problems

**Frequency:** Strong evidence in Kath Pay transcript

**Evidence:**
- Kath Pay SaaS case study: A SaaS company was emailing people
  who took a free trial 5 years ago and never converted.
  Result: chronic deliverability problems because recipients
  kept hitting spam. The fix was not technical — it was
  strategic. Stop emailing people who are not interested.
- Kath Pay: "You are always going to have deliverability problems
  if you send emails that are not wanted."

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
Deliverability is a symptom, not the root problem. The root
problem is sending email to people who did not ask for it or
have lost interest. List hygiene and permission-based email
is not just ethical — it is technically necessary for inbox
placement.

---

## Pattern 08 — AI helps execution but not strategy

**Frequency:** Mentioned explicitly by 2/8 experts

**Evidence:**
- Kath Pay: "AI is clever but not clever enough to identify
  whether advice is regurgitated rubbish or actually true. It
  can only work with what it is being fed."
- Kath Pay: "I train my AI on my own beliefs and frameworks in
  email marketing, not on external beliefs. That is the key."

**Implication for B2B SaaS:**
AI email tools can draft copy, suggest subject lines, and
automate sequences. But the strategic layer — who to email,
when, with what value proposition, and why — still requires
human judgment. Companies that let AI replace strategy will
produce high-volume, low-impact email programs.

---

## Summary: The Core Tension in B2B SaaS Email

Every expert in this research is navigating the same tension:

**What management wants:**
More sends. More CTAs. More revenue attribution. Faster.

**What actually works:**
Fewer sends to the right people. More value. Longer time
horizon. Consistent presence. Trust before conversion.

The practitioners who resolve this tension — by building
business cases, running A/B tests, and documenting results
— are the ones who build email programs that compound over
time rather than decay.
